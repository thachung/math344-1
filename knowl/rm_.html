Remark 1.2.2. Equivalent alternative to axiom (2).

Axiom (2) in DefinitionÂ 1.2.1 is equivalent to saying that \(B_1\cap B_2\) is equal to a union of elements of \(\mathcal{B}\text{.}\) In other words, we could replace (2) with the equivalent statement
\begin{equation*} (2') B_1, B_2\in \mathcal{B}\implies B_1\cap B_2=\bigcup_{\alpha\in I} B_\alpha \text{ for some } B_\alpha\in \mathcal{B}\text{.} \end{equation*}
This is a consequence of the following more general fact: let \(U\) be a subset of \(X\text{,}\) and let \(\mathcal{A}\) be a collection of subsets of \(X\text{;}\) we have \(U=\bigcup_{i\in I} A_i\) for some \(A_i\in \mathcal{A}\) if and only if for all \(x\in U\) there exists an element \(A_x\in \mathcal{A}\) such that \(x\in A_x\subseteq U\text{.}\) I'll leave the proof of this to you.
