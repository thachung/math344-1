Item 3.

Assume \(U_1, U_2, \dots, U_n\in \mathcal{T}\text{,}\) and let \(U=\bigcap_{i=1}^nU_i\text{.}\) We have
\begin{align*} x\in U \amp\implies x\in U_i \text{ for all } 1\leq i\leq n \\ \amp\implies \forall i \exists \epsilon_i > 0 \text{ s.t. }(x-\epsilon_i, x+\epsilon)\subseteq U_i \\ \amp \implies (x-\epsilon, x+\epsilon)\subseteq U_i \text{ for all } i, \text{ where } \epsilon=\min \{\epsilon_i\colon 1\leq i\leq n\}\\\ \amp \implies (x-\epsilon, x+\epsilon)\subseteq U \text{.} \end{align*}
This shows that \(U\) satisfies the defining property of \(\mathcal{T}\text{,}\) and hence that \(U\in \mathcal{T}\text{.}\)
