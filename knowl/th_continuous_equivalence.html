Theorem 1.9.7. Continuity equivalences.

Let \(f\colon X\rightarrow Y\) be a function between the topological spaces \(X, Y\text{.}\) The following statements are equivalent.
  1. \(f\) is continuous.
  2. For all \(A\subseteq X\text{,}\) we have \(f(\overline{A})\subseteq \overline{f(A)}\text{.}\)
  3. For all closed sets \(C\subseteq Y\text{,}\) the preimage \(f^{-1}(C)\) is closed in \(X\text{.}\)
  4. For all \(x\in X\) and open neighborhood \(V\ni x\text{,}\) there is an open neighborhood \(U\ni x\) such that \(f(U)\subseteq V\text{.}\)
