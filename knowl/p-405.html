Let \(X_1, X_2,\dots, X_n\) be topological spaces, and let
\begin{equation*} X=\prod_{i=1}X_i=X_1\times X_2\times \cdots \times X_n=\{(x_1,x_2,\dots, x_n)\colon x_i\in X_i\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
The collection
\begin{equation*} \mathcal{B}=\{U_1\times U_2\times \cdots \times U_n\colon U_i \text{ open in } X_i\} \end{equation*}
is a basis on \(X\text{.}\) The topology \(\mathcal{T}\) generated by \(\mathcal{B}\) is the product topology on \(X\text{,}\) and \(X\) is called the product of the spaces \(X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n\text{.}\)
