Assume \(U_i\in \mathcal{T}\) for all \(i\in I\text{,}\) and let \(U=\bigcup_{i\in I}U_i\text{.}\) We have
\begin{align*} x\in U \amp \implies x\in U_i \text{ for some } i\in I\\ \amp \implies (x-\epsilon, x+\epsilon)\subseteq U_i \text{ for some } i\in I, \epsilon > 0\\ \amp \implies (x-\epsilon, x+\epsilon)\subseteq U \text{ for some } i\in I, \epsilon > 0 \text{.} \end{align*}
This shows \(U\) satisfies the defining proerty of \(\mathcal{T}\text{,}\) and hence \(U\in \mathcal{T}\text{.}\)
