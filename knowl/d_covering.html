Definition 1.15.1. Covering.

Let \(Y\) be a subset of the topological space \(X\text{.}\) A collection \(\mathcal{A}=\{A_i\}_{i\in I}\) is a covering of \(Y\) if \(Y\subseteq \bigcup_{i\in I} A_i\text{.}\) As a shorthand, we will often say in this case that \(Y=\bigcup_{i\in I}A_i\) is a covering. Note in particular that a covering of \(X\) itself is a collection of sets \(\{A_i\}_{i\in I}\) satisfying \(X=\bigcup_{i\in I} A_i\text{.}\)
Given a property of subsets (e.g., open, closed, etc.), a covering \(\mathcal{A}=\{A_i\}_{i\in I}\) is said to satisfy this property if \(A_i\in \mathcal{A}\) does for all \(i\in I\text{.}\)
