Proof

Assume by contradiction that \(X=U_1\cup U_2\) is a separation of \(X\text{.}\) We can find points \(x_1\in U_1, x_2\in U_2\text{.}\) Let \(f\colon [a,b]\rightarrow X\) be a path connecting \(x, x'\text{.}\) By Theorem 1.12.6, the image \(f([a.b])\) is connected. By Theorem 1.12.5 we must have \(f([a,b])\subseteq U_1\) or \(f([a,b])\subseteq U_2\text{.}\) This contradicts the fact that \(f(a)=x_1\in U_1, f(b)=x_2\in U_2\) and \(U_1\cap U_2=\emptyset\text{.}\) Thus there can be no separation of \(X\text{,}\) and we conclude that it is connected.
