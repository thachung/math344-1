Let \(X=\R^n\text{.}\) Recall that the Euclidean metric let \(d\) and box metric \(\rho\) both induce the Euclidean topology on \(\R^n\text{.}\) For any \(A\subseteq X\) the following conditions are equivalent.
  1. \(A\) is compact.
  2. \(A\) is closed and bounded with respect to \(d\) (equivalently, with respect to \(\rho\)).
