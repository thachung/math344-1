Let \((S^*, g^*)\) be a maximal element of \(\mathcal{F}\text{,}\) so that \(g^*\) is a limit point of the net \((f_i\vert_{S^*})_{i\in I}\text{.}\) We show that \(S^*\) and hence that \(g^*=f\in X\) is a limit point of \((f_i)_{i\in I}\text{.}\)
Assume by contradiction that \(S^*\ne T\text{,}\) and let \(t_0\in T-S^*\text{.}\) Since \(X_{t_0}\) is compact, by LemmaÂ 1.25.5 the net \((f_i\vert_{S^*\cup \{t_0}})_{i\in I}\) in \(X_{S^*\cup {t_0}}=X_{S^*}\times X_{t_0}\) has a limit point of the form \((g^*, x_{t_0})\text{.}\) But then the element \((S,g)\in \mathcal{F}\) defined as \(S=S^*\cup \{t_0\}\) and
\begin{equation*} g(s)=\begin{cases} g^*(s)\amp \text{if } s\in S^*\\ x_{t_0}\amp \text{if } s=t_0 \end{equation*}
satisfies \((S,g)\geq (S^*, g^*)\) and \((S,g)\ne (S^*, g^*)\text{,}\) contradicting the fact that \((S^*, g^*)\) is a maximal element.
