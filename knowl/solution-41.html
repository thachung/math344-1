Solution 1.17.5.1.

  1. The map \(\iota\colon (-\pi/2,\pi/2)\hookrightarrow S^1\) defined as \(\iota(t)=(\cos 2t, \sin 2t)\) is an open embedding of \((-\pi/2, \pi/2)\) onto \(S^1-\{(-1,0)\}\text{.}\) Thus this is a realization of the one-point compactification of \((-\pi/2, \pi/2)\text{.}\)
  2. Since \(\arctan\colon \R\rightarrow (-\pi/2,\pi/2)\) is a homeomorphism, the map \(\lambda\) defined as the composition
    \begin{equation*} \R\xrightarrow{\arctan} (\pi/2,\pi/2)\xrightarrow{\iota} S^1 \end{equation*}
    is an open embedding of \(\R\) onto \(S^1-\{(-1,0)\}\text{,}\) providing a realization of the one-point compactification of \(\R\text{.}\) Note that we have by definition \(\lambda(t)=(\cos(2\arctan t), \sin(2\arctan t))\text{.}\)
  3. Since the inclusion \(i\colon \Z\hookrightarrow \R\) is a closed embedding, the restriction
    \begin{align*} \lambda\vert_\Z\colon \Z \amp \hookrightarrow S^1\\ n \amp \mapsto (\cos(2\arctan n),\sin(2\arctan n)) \end{align*}
    is an embedding (neither open nor closed) of \(\Z\) into \(S^1\text{.}\) Restricting the codomain to the closed (hence compact) subset \(Y=\lambda(\Z)\cup\{(-1,0)\}\) we get a one-point compactification
    \begin{equation*} \Z\hookrightarrow \{(-1,0)\}\cup \{(\cos(2\arctan n),\sin(2\arctan n))\colon n\in \Z\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
