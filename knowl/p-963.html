Let \(X, Y\) be topological spaces, and assume \(Y\) is compact. Let \(f=((x_i, y_i))_{i\in I}\) is a net in \(X\times Y\text{.}\) Let \(\pi_X\colon X\times Y\rightarrow X\) be the projection onto \(X\) map. If \(x\in X\) is a limit point of the net \(\pi_X\circ f=(x_i)_{i\in I}\text{,}\) then there is a \(y\in Y\) such that \((x,y)\) is a limit point of \(f\text{.}\)
