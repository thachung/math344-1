Topological specimen 2. Trivial and discrete topology.

Let \(X\) be a set.
  1. The collection \(\mathcal{T}=\{\emptyset, X\}\) is a topology on \(X\text{,}\) called the trivial topology. This is the topology on \(X\) whose only open sets are the empty set and \(X\) itself.
  2. The collection \(\mathcal{T}=\mathcal{P}(X)\) is a topology on \(X\text{,}\) called the discrete topology. This is the topology on \(X\) for which every subset of \(X\) is an open set.
