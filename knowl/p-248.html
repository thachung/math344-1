In this section we introduce a special family of topological spaces: metric spaces. These types of spaces are pervasive throughout mathematics and are conceptually very familiar or intuitive. Indeed, historically metric spaces were the main inspiration to axiomatic topology; and the metric balls defined in DefinitionÂ 1.3.3 were the inspiration for the notion of a topological basis. Lastly, the defining structure of metric space, a metric \(d\text{,}\) gives us a quantitative grip on the topology, wheareas generally in topology we must rely on the qualitative description of open sets given by the axioms.
