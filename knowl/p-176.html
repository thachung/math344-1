When \(X=\{a\}\text{,}\) we have \(\mathcal{P}(X)=\{\emptyset, \{a\}\}\text{.}\) Since by axiom (1) a topology \(\mathcal{T}\) on \(X\) must contain both \(\emptyset\) and \(X=\{a\}\text{,}\) we see the only option is \(\mathcal{T}=\{\emptyset, \{a\}\}\text{.}\) It is now easy to see that \(\{\emptyset, \{a\}\}\) satisfies axioms (2) and (3).
