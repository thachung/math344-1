Assume \(X\) is countable. A nonempty open set of \(X\) is of the form \(U=X-\{x_1,x_2,\dots, x_n\}\) for some \(n\text{.}\) It follows that the nonempty open sets of \(X\) are in bijection with the set of all finite subsets of \(X\text{.}\) By RemarkÂ 1.18.3, this collection is countable. Thus \(X\) has countably many open sets. Thus for any \(x\in X\) the set of all open sets containing \(x\) is countable.
