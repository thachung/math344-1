Definition 1.16.3. Bounded, diameter, and distance to sets.

Let \((X,d)\) be a metric space, let \(A\subseteq X\) be a nonempty set, and let \(x\in X\text{.}\)
The diameter of \(A\text{,}\) denoted \(\operatorname{diam} A\text{,}\) is defined as \(\operatorname{diam} A=\sup\{d(x,y)\colon x, y\in A\}\text{.}\) The set \(A\) is bounded if \(\operatorname{diam} A < \infty\text{.}\)
The distance \(d(x,A)\) from \(x\) to \(A\) is defined as \(d(x,A)=\inf\{d(x,y)\colon y\in A\}\text{.}\)
